Streaming TV

Programming is provided through YouTube TV for network and cable channels, and Netflix Premium for movies and other programming. Netflix Premium permits viewing on up to four devices at once.

  1. Turn on the desired TV.
  2. Navigate to the TV home screen.
  3. Select YouTube TV or Netflix. These should be near the top of the app list unless moved by a previous guest.
  4. Each app should be logged into the Serenity Summit account. If not, contact the rental company.
Please do not rearrange or remove these two primary apps, and do not sign out of either account.

Games

An assortment of games is provided on both the main and lower levels. Please leave them in good condition for future guests.

The cabin includes convenient power and charging ports attached to various pieces of furniture. Many table lamps also have USB charging ports in their bases.

The cabin is not connected to a municipal sewer system. Wastewater is processed by a dedicated septic system designed only for wastewater, toilet paper, and material that has passed through the digestive system.

Do not put anything else into drains or toilets. A septic failure can cause a wastewater backup and extremely expensive repairs.

Never flush or drain

  • Diapers
  • Wipes, including “flushable” wipes
  • Hygiene products
  • Paper towels
  • Solid or undigested food
  • Coffee grounds
  • Grease or oil
  • Chemicals or paint

Thank you for helping protect the system and the stays of future guests.

The cabin is supplied by a private well rather than a municipal water system. The well pump depends on electricity.

During a power failure, water may not be available. This can also limit the ability to flush toilets.

Contact the rental management company if you experience water-supply issues.

Hot water

The cabin is equipped with an 80-gallon electric water heater. With a larger party, manage hot-water use among showers, baths, the washing machine and the dishwasher.

Using several simultaneously may overwhelm the heater’s recovery capacity. Contact the rental management company if the problem persists.

North Carolina’s High Country is bear country. Bears may visit Serenity Summit while looking for a meal.

Do not leave out trash, food coolers or pet food. It encourages repeat visits and can result in trash being scattered across the property.

Dispose of trash in the bear-proof receptacle near the front shed

Insert your index finger in the latch and pull outward in a “come here” motion. Put bagged trash inside one of the containers and securely close the lid to reduce odors.

Bears are wild animals. Mother bears are highly protective of their cubs. Everyone in your party, including pets, should maintain a safe distance.

The Viking Regal ETS spa is located on the lower patio and is maintained by the rental management company.

Spa rules

  • Use at your own risk.
  • Always supervise minors and children.
  • Consider medical conditions for which hot-tub use is not advised.
  • Shower before use.
  • Do not add soap, bubble bath or similar products.
  • No glass containers. Plastic tumblers are provided in both kitchens.
  • Close the cover whenever the spa is not in use.

The spa will run occasionally to circulate, filter and heat the water. This is normal. It has two jet pumps; use either or both as needed. The jets automatically shut off after a preset time and can simply be restarted.

All jets can be adjusted from fully off to fully on. Two knobs control the amount of bubbling.

Filling

The main-level Jacuzzi tub holds 60 gallons and can use most of the cabin’s available hot water. Avoid using other tubs, showers, washing machines or dishwashers while it fills.

Filling can take up to 20 minutes. Monitor it closely even though it has an overflow drain. Remember that the water level rises when you enter.

Do not turn on the jets until the lower-front intake is fully submerged. Turn the tub off before draining.

Loft fan and light

The wall panel controls the light and fan speed. Pressing the additional timer button while the light is on makes the light flash once, then turn off in about one minute—enough time to get into bed or head downstairs.

Gas grills should be available on the deck and lower patio.

  1. Always supervise children and minors.
  2. Open the grill lid.
  3. Confirm the propane tank has fuel; replace it with a spare if needed.
  4. Fully open the propane valve by turning it counter-clockwise.
  5. Turn on the designated ignition burner while pressing and holding the igniter. You should hear clicking and the burner should ignite.
  6. Turn on other burners as desired, set the heat level, close the lid and allow the grill to preheat.
If the grill does not ignite, turn the burner knob off and allow propane gas to clear before troubleshooting or trying again.

Clean the grill after each use with the provided brush. Contact the rental management company if you need additional propane.

The stove’s damper controls how quickly wood burns and how much heat is generated.

Fully open: pull the control all the way toward you, typically when first starting the fire.

Close: push the control inward until you reach the desired burn rate.

Never discard ashes that may still be hot. Use the provided shovel and ash can, and protect the wood floor and furniture from hot coals.

The faux fireplaces provide visual ambiance and supplemental heat.

Main-level TV console fireplace

Use the remote or buttons at the upper right of the fireplace to control power, flame brightness, flame speed, down-light level, heat level and shut-off timer.

Downstairs fireplace

Use the remote or buttons at the upper right to control power, flame intensity, heat level and shut-off timer.

Bunk-room heater

The heater can operate with flames only, low heat (1000W), or high heat (1500W). Use the thermostat knob for additional temperature control and the right knob for flame intensity.

Keep the bunk-room heater unplugged when not in use.

Dining-room radiant baseboard heater

The control knob is on the left. Because there is no on-indicator light, remember to turn it off when not needed and on departure day.

Nearby residents live here full-time or part-time. Please respect their privacy and keep after-hours noise reasonable.

Cats and dogs

Local pets treat the secluded hill as their playground. Tufts may stop by for attention. A wary black cat sometimes appears near an abandoned barn. Sable is large but typically friendly, and another wolf-like neighborhood dog has also been reported as friendly. Use your judgment, especially with your own pets.

Wild animals

You may see groundhogs, deer, wild turkeys and black bears. At night you may hear coyotes. Enjoy the wildlife, but maintain distance and treat every wild animal with respect.

Edgar Tufts was an important figure in Banner Elk’s history, particularly in education and community development.

Education and community

Tufts founded Banner Elk Academy in 1884 and worked to improve educational opportunities in the Appalachian region. He also helped strengthen community institutions and infrastructure.

Tourism and legacy

He promoted Banner Elk as a summer retreat, emphasizing its natural beauty, fresh air and mild climate. His work helped shape the town’s future. He died in 1923, but his influence remains visible in the institutions and community he helped build.
